About

I'm a product manager with a background in biomedical engineering — which means I approach every product problem by asking what does the user actually need to do, and why aren't they able to do it today?

At Medis Medical Imaging, I've worked across the full PM spectrum: from building a 0→1 global learning platform to driving early US market development for a cardiology imaging tool. My work sits at the crossroads of clinical workflows, enterprise stakeholders, and software delivery.

I believe the best products are built through relentless discovery, honest metrics, and tight collaboration between design, engineering, and the people using the product every day.

Principle 01
Start with the user's job
Features don't create value — outcomes do. I anchor every decision in what the user is trying to accomplish, not what's technically possible.
Principle 02
Measure what matters
Adoption metrics, onboarding efficiency, lead generation — I define success criteria before writing a single spec.
Principle 03
Build for scale from day one
Whether it's 10 users or 10,000, the architecture of a product — technical and organizational — determines how far it can grow.
Principle 04
Influence without authority
Cross-functional alignment is a product skill. I build trust with engineers, clinicians, and C-suite stakeholders by understanding their language.
